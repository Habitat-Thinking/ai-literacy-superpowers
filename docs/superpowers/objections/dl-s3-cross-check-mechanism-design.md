# Adversarial Review — dl-s3-cross-check-mechanism-design (spec mode)

Spec under review: `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-05-29-dl-s3-cross-check-mechanism-design.md`. Mode: `spec`. Reviewer: `claude-opus-4-7[1m]`. Ten objections raised: 5 high (O1–O5), 4 medium (O6–O9), 1 low (O10).

Distribution across categories: 1 premise, 1 scope, 4 implementation, 3 specification quality, 1 alternatives, 0 risk (deprioritised at spec time per the skill).

The spec is structurally faithful to its S2b precedent: the choice of option (a), the prefix-on-existing-field schema reuse, the dimension-mirroring direction-flavoured weighting, and the sentinel parallel are all defended by analogy. The weakness is that **the analogy is doing more work than the evidence**: S2b named per-element failure modes and operationalised weighting against them (O1); the asymmetric-input no-op is recorded at element granularity for a model-level fact (O9); the bidirectional side-effect path quietly violates the single-source-of-truth assumption the audit-trail contract rests on (O3); and the three-mode dispatcher contract has at least one mode (`construct-only`) whose only consumer is the hypothetical "v0.3.0 exactly" case the spec invents to justify it (O2). The ordering contract `CC<N> follows Q<N>` is asserted at three layers and verified at none (O5).

These are not blockers for shipping (a). They are honest debts the spec should either pay before merge or name as open and revisitable on disposition signal.
